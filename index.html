Zoo Animals

    Bear

    1. Ollie
    2. Mona

    A bear is a carnivoran mammal belonging to the family Ursidae, characterized by their large bodies, stocky legs, long snouts, and shaggy hair.

    Giraffe

    1. Frankie
    2. Coconut

    A large African hoofed mammal belonging to the genus Giraffa. It is the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant on Earth, known for its extremely long neck and legs, horn-like ossicones, and spotted coat patterns.

    Lion

    1. Mella
    2. Karl

    A large cat native to Africa and India, known for its muscular, broad-chested body, short, rounded head, round ears, and a dark, hairy tuft at the tip of its tail.

    Monkey

    1. Cookie
    2. Earl
    3. Banana Pudding

    A nonhuman primate mammal, typically excluding lemurs and tarsiers, and is part of the Simiiformes infraorder. Monkeys are known for their agility, intelligence, and social behavior, often living in trees and forming complex social structures.

    Alligator

    1. Wren
    2. Aspen
    3. Mika

    A large reptile in the genus Alligator, classified in the family Alligatoridae and order Crocodilia. Alligators can be distinguished from true crocodiles by their broad, U-shaped snout and the fact that the large fourth tooth in the lower jaw fits into a socket in the upper jaw and is not visible when the mouth is closed.
